I was also stuck for a while trying to optimize the explicit 3×30 selectors, but that turned out to be the wrong object to optimize.

The useful reframing was that the output layout is not really a “layout tensor”. It is a relation between a few small input indices and the final output indices. Once I treated it that way, I could generate [1,10,30,30] directly (based on 13th's comment) and avoid the expensive intermediate selector/materialized layout entirely.

So my hint would be.. don’t look for a special opset trick first. Opset 18 is fine. Look for an ONNX operator/formulation where the routing relation is represented compactly as parameters/initializers, rather than as explicit 3×30 masks or selectors.

If you are still carrying large selector tensors, even sparse-looking ones, the cost is probably being spent before the real computation starts.

By the way, I reached further (cost : 90) with this method in this task. Good luck!
