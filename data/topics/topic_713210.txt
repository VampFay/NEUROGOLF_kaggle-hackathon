Looking for tips to solve more tasks — currently at 66/400 with hand-crafted + MLP approach

Hand-crafted ONNX graphs for clean patterns (recolor, rotation, flip, transpose, mirror, scale, mode-fill)
A small 2-layer MLP (hidden 4-64) trained on all examples including arc-gen, exported to opset-10 ONNX via Gemm+Relu+Gemm My MLP only handles tasks where all examples share the same input/output shape. MLPs are a dead end here. On any 7k+ task, an MLP that actually learns ends up 10–40× more expensive than a symbolic graph — and at cost-feasible sizes it doesn't learn at all. The grader needs full-grid exactness, so partial accuracy scores zero. No task is both learnable and cheaper than baseline.

Why stuck on opset-10? It's not required. Recent opsets load fine, and opset ≥14 unlocks cheap uint8 ops that roughly halve memory cost.

Flood-fill: unroll fixed steps — each step reach = Min(MaxPool3x3(reach), passable), repeated N times (N = family diameter). Enclosed regions = flood from the border, then passable AND NOT reach.
